I had a blocker today.

My microphone stopped working in Claude Code. Every time I held the spacebar to talk, the screen said "listening" but nothing came through. My first instinct was to look for a new fix. Update the version. Try a different mic. Read the latest changelog.

And then Claude told me something I have been telling him for weeks: check the session logs.

We had already solved this.

Not the exact same issue, but the category. A while back I had a different audio problem and the fix was a permissions reset. That note was sitting in my own session log. The answer was in my own notebook, and I almost skipped it to go hunt for a new one.

If I'm being honest, this is not just about microphones.

I do this a lot. I run into something hard at work, or in my marriage, or in my Bible reading, and my first instinct is to look outside for a new answer when the answer was already in something I wrote down three weeks ago. I forget what I have already learned. And so I re-learn it, slower this time, usually with more frustration.

The Lord has been so kind to give me the habit of writing things down. A journal. A session log. A quote in a notebook. A verse I underlined. These are little mercies. They are gifts from the memory of someone who was paying attention, to the future self who will forget.

It is a good reminder that wisdom is often not new. Wisdom is old and already written. The question is whether I read my own work before I go looking for someone else's.

So I am going to keep writing. Small notes. Rough ones. Not because they are polished, but because some version of me in three weeks will need them.

And maybe, if I leave the door open, one of them might help you too.

— Lala
