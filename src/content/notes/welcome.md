I keep a lot of notes. Some of them are tactical — how a prompt pattern worked,
why a workflow broke, what I'd do differently next time. Some of them are
quieter — a verse that caught me that morning, a hard conversation I'm still
thinking about, a decision I didn't know how to make yet.

For a long time, those notes lived in scattered places. A draft folder. A
spreadsheet. A notebook on the desk. LinkedIn posts that scrolled away after
a week. The pieces that helped someone — the ones that helped me — kept
disappearing because they had nowhere to come home to.

This site is that home.

It's not a blog. It's not a portfolio. It's not a ministry site. It's a
content library, built to grow for decades, where guides live next to stories,
stories live next to field notes, and field notes live next to reflections
that didn't really want to be essays.

If you're here because you're trying to build an AI-powered content system
and you want to see what's working in mine — welcome. If you're here because
you're living with chronic illness and trying to keep working faithfully
anyway — welcome. If you're here because you're a Filipino freelancer
trying to figure out what a global career looks like from home — welcome.
If you're here because you love the Lord and you want to see someone trying
to follow Him through a normal weekday — welcome.

Nothing here is polished. That's on purpose.

The plan is to write the things I wish someone had written down for me,
and to keep writing them until they add up to something useful.

Start wherever. Leave whenever. Come back when something nudges you to.

— Lala
