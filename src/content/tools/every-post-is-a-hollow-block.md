The content you post should not just be whatever you happened to think of that day.

Every piece of content has to be relevant to the bigger picture. To what you are actually trying to say to your audience.

Here is what I mean.

Every piece of content you put on social media is a building block. Like a hollow block. When you build a house, the hollow blocks are the structure. Your content is the structure of the thing you are building with your audience. How you engage, what you talk about, what you leave out. Those are the bricks.

If your message framework is not clear, if you do not know how to communicate clearly with your clients or your audience, it will be very hard to create content that actually engages.

That is why you have to practice writing.

You cannot understand this in one sitting. You need to be saturated in it. Marinated in it. You have to know what the purpose of your business is. The purpose of your services. The purpose of your products. What your audience is going through. What your customers are going through. What they want to see. What they want to know.

Only then can you create relevant content. Building blocks that actually build something.

I see people posting every day and wondering why their audience is not growing. It is not because you do not post often enough. It is because the posts are not connected to anything bigger than the post itself.

So before you ask, "what should I post today?" ask the slower question first.

What am I trying to build?

Then pick the block.
