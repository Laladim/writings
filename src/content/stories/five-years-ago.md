Five years ago, I was broke, broken, and lost.

It was not just that I had no money and no work. I had serious health conditions. I had a tumor in a vein in my brain. I had no spleen and no gallbladder anymore. I had degenerative disc disease.

Imagine having the spine of a sixty-year-old grandmother when you are only thirty.

But the hardest part? I did not know who I was or where I was going.

Those days were really dark. You know that feeling when three things hit you all at once. No money, no job, and you are sick on top of it. It is so hard to get up.

I was so lost I kept changing my hair color. From blonde to red to violet to pink. I was trying to find myself.

Every night I would cry, hoping someone would tell me, "you can do this, you are not alone."

It felt like everyone else was moving forward while I was a lost child walking on the side of the street.

Instead of letting the situation defeat me, I made it my mission to find my identity through freelancing. And if I am being honest, my first reason was not noble. I needed to earn despite my health. I am not going to be a hypocrite about that.

But I learned something along the way. You cannot give what you do not have. So I focused first on building myself. Learning digital marketing. Getting certifications. Slowly building my expertise.

Looking back, the promise of the Lord is true. Our sufferings are His blessings in disguise. Those struggles were not meant to break me. They were meant to build my character and prepare me for a greater purpose.

"Suffering yet always rejoicing," the Bible says.

When we have nothing, that is when we learn who actually loves us. And that is when we realize we are just a small dot in God's vast universe.

Today I am a Certified Facebook Blueprint Creative Strategist, a Google Educator, and a community leader. My story is not just about career transformation. It is about finding your true identity.

And it is not easy. But it is worth it.

For the glory of God.

— Lala
